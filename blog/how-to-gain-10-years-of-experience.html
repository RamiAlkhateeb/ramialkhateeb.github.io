How to gain 10 years of experience in a domain beyond your career

Practical steps to master a field that isn’t part of your career

The phrase “If you can’t explain it simply, you don’t understand it well enough” is a widely attributed quote to Albert Einstein, reflecting the idea that a deep understanding of a concept allows one to break it down into simple, understandable terms, and that complexity in explanation often stems from a lack of genuine comprehension.

In 2017, I was doing freelance work, which involved doing research about the semantic web, and then I did a course in machine learning because my graduation project was about recommendation systems.

I felt that I liked data science related fields, and I improved my knowledge about data analysis and statistics over the years by doing courses or reading books from time to time. Later, and before I make any serious career shift (thankfully), I discovered through the steps that I’ve mentioned, that liking something does not necessarily mean that it’s my place.

Through the steps that I’ve mentioned: volunteering, teaching, mentoring, etc., you will gain experience, and you will find positive or negative answers to your questions, which will help you get over your doubts.
